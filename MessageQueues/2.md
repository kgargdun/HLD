# Message Queues — Delivery Semantics (Interview Core)

> ⚠️ This is one of the **most asked topics in HLD**.
> If you get this right, you stand out.

---

## 1. Why Delivery Semantics Matter

In distributed systems:

* Network failures
* Consumer crashes
* Broker retries

👉 Question:

> **What guarantees do we give about message delivery?**

---

## 2. At-Most-Once Delivery

### Definition

> Message is delivered **0 or 1 time** (never duplicated)

---

### How it works

* Broker sends message
* No retry if failure

---

### Behavior

```id="h2k9pl"
Send → Consumer crash → Message lost ❌
```

---

### Pros

* No duplicates
* Simple
* Fast

---

### Cons

* Message loss possible ❗

---

### Use Cases

* Logs, metrics (loss acceptable)

---

## 3. At-Least-Once Delivery ⭐ (MOST COMMON)

### Definition

> Message is delivered **at least once (≥1)**
> → May be duplicated

---

### How it works

* Broker retries until ACK received

---

### Behavior

```id="q8zn21"
Send → Consumer processes → crash before ACK
→ Broker retries → Duplicate processing ❗
```

---

### Pros

* No message loss
* Reliable

---

### Cons

* Duplicate messages

---

### Use Cases

* Payments, orders (loss NOT acceptable)

---

## 4. Exactly-Once Delivery ⚠️ (TRICKY)

### Definition (Ideal)

> Message is delivered **exactly once** (no loss, no duplicates)

---

### Reality

> ❗ True exactly-once is **extremely hard** in distributed systems

---

### Why?

* Network retries
* Consumer crashes
* Partial failures

---

### What systems actually do

* Combine:

  * At-least-once delivery
  * * Idempotent processing

---

## 5. Idempotency (VERY IMPORTANT)

### Definition

> Processing the same message multiple times produces the same result

---

### Example

```id="z1pl8k"
Charge ₹100 → BAD (duplicate charge)
Set balance = ₹100 → GOOD (idempotent)
```

---

### Techniques

#### (a) Idempotency Key

* Unique request ID

---

#### (b) Deduplication Store

* Track processed messages

---

#### (c) Upserts

* Replace instead of insert

---

### Interview Insight

> “Exactly-once is usually achieved via idempotent consumers on top of at-least-once delivery.”

---

## 6. ACK Handling

### Flow

```id="r8q2ml"
1. Broker sends message
2. Consumer processes
3. Consumer sends ACK
4. Broker deletes message
```

---

### Failure Case

```id="y7bn4s"
Process done → ACK lost → retry → duplicate
```

---

👉 Root cause of duplicates

---

## 7. Visibility Timeout (Important Concept)

Used in systems like Amazon SQS

---

### Idea

* Message becomes invisible after being picked
* If not ACKed in time → reappears

---

### Purpose

* Handle consumer crashes

---

## 8. Deduplication Strategies

### (a) Producer-side

* Avoid sending duplicates

---

### (b) Broker-side

* Dedup within time window

---

### (c) Consumer-side (MOST COMMON)

* Track processed IDs

---

## 9. Tradeoff Summary

| Guarantee     | Loss  | Duplicate | Complexity |
| ------------- | ----- | --------- | ---------- |
| At-most-once  | ❌ Yes | ✅ No      | Low        |
| At-least-once | ✅ No  | ❌ Yes     | Medium     |
| Exactly-once  | ❌ No  | ❌ No      | Very High  |

---

## 10. What to Say in Interviews (IMPORTANT)

### Golden Answer

> “Most systems use at-least-once delivery with idempotent consumers to achieve effectively exactly-once processing.”

---

### If pushed further

* Mention:

  * Deduplication
  * Idempotency keys
  * Transactional writes (Kafka)

---

## 11. Common Follow-ups

* How do you prevent duplicate payments?

---

## 12. Real-World Insight

* Apache Kafka:

  * Supports idempotent producers + transactions
* Amazon SQS:

  * At-least-once + visibility timeout

---

## 13. One-Line Summary

> At-least-once delivery is the industry standard, and exactly-once is typically achieved via idempotent processing on top of it.
