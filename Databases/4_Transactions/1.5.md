# ⚙️ 1.5 TRANSACTIONS (ACID + ISOLATION)

---

## 📘 Definition (textbook-style)

A **transaction** is a sequence of database operations that are executed as a single logical unit of work, ensuring consistency and correctness even in the presence of failures or concurrent access.

---

## 💡 Intuition (Why it exists)

Real-world operations are not single queries:

👉 Example:

* Deduct money from A
* Add money to B

If one succeeds and the other fails → ❌ inconsistent system

> Transactions ensure “all or nothing” behavior.

---

## 🧩 ACID Properties (CORE)

---

## 1. Atomicity

---

### 📘 Definition

All operations in a transaction either **complete fully or none at all**

---

### 💡 Example

* Debit succeeds, credit fails → rollback everything

---

### 🧠 Intuition

> No partial updates

---

## 2. Consistency

---

### 📘 Definition

Database moves from one **valid state to another valid state**

---

### 💡 Example

* Balance cannot become negative (constraint)

---

### 🧠 Intuition

> Rules must always hold

---

## 3. Isolation (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

### 📘 Definition

Concurrent transactions should not interfere with each other

---

### 💡 Problem

Multiple users updating same data simultaneously

---

## 4. Durability

---

### 📘 Definition

Once committed, data is **permanently stored**, even after crashes

---

### 💡 Achieved via

* WAL (Write-Ahead Log)

---

## ⚖️ Isolation Levels (VERY HIGH YIELD)

---

## 🔹 1. Read Uncommitted

* Can see uncommitted data
* ❌ Dirty reads possible

---

## 🔹 2. Read Committed

* Only sees committed data
* ✅ No dirty reads
* ❌ Non-repeatable reads possible

---

## 🔹 3. Repeatable Read

* Same query → same result
* ❌ Phantom reads possible

---

## 🔹 4. Serializable

* Full isolation (like sequential execution)
* ✅ Strongest guarantee
* ❌ Slowest

---

## ⚠️ Common Anomalies

---

### 1. Dirty Read

* Reading uncommitted data

---

### 2. Non-repeatable Read

* Same query → different result

---

### 3. Phantom Read

* New rows appear in query

---

## 🌍 Real-world Mapping

---

* PostgreSQL → strong ACID
* MySQL

---

### NoSQL systems:

* Cassandra → weaker transactions
* DynamoDB → limited transactions

---

## ⚖️ Trade-offs

---

| Property         | Trade-off                         |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Strong isolation | Lower concurrency, higher latency |
| Weak isolation   | Better performance, anomalies     |

---

## 🎯 When to use vs not use

---

### Use strong transactions when:

* Financial systems
* Inventory management
* Critical data consistency

---

### Avoid heavy transactions when:

* High scale system
* Eventual consistency acceptable

👉 Use:

* Denormalization
* Idempotent operations

---

## 🧠 Key Insight (INTERVIEW GOLD)

> “Transactions ensure correctness, but strong isolation reduces scalability — large systems often relax isolation for performance.”

---

## 🗣️ Interview One-liner

> “Transactions provide ACID guarantees to ensure correctness, with isolation levels balancing consistency and performance in concurrent systems.”

---

## 🚨 Red Flags / Mistakes

* ❌ Assuming all DBs support full ACID
* ❌ Using serializable everywhere (kills performance)
* ❌ Ignoring isolation levels
* ❌ Not understanding anomalies
* ❌ Overusing transactions in distributed systems

---

## 🔥 Advanced Insight (VERY IMPORTANT)

At scale:

👉 Systems avoid distributed transactions

Instead use:

* Eventual consistency
* Sagas
* Idempotency

---

## ✍️ Quick Mental Model

> ACID = Correctness guarantees
>
> Isolation level = performance vs correctness trade-off
