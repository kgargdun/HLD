# DATABASE SYSTEM PATTERNS (LEVEL 5)

---

## 🎯 GOAL

This section answers:

> “How are databases actually used in real systems?”

---

## 🧠 CORE IDEA

Different systems → different dominant constraints

👉 So database usage patterns change accordingly

---

# 🧩 1. READ-HEAVY SYSTEMS

---

## 💡 Examples
- News feed
- Product catalog
- Content platforms

---

## ⚙️ Characteristics
- Reads >> Writes
- Low latency required
- High QPS

---

## 🏗️ Architecture

User →
- Cache (Redis)
- Read replicas
- Primary DB

---

## 🚀 Techniques

### 1. Caching (CRITICAL)
- Cache hot data
- Reduce DB load

---

### 2. Read Replicas
- Scale reads horizontally

---

### 3. Denormalization
- Avoid joins
- Faster reads

---

## ⚠️ Problems

- Cache invalidation
- Replica lag

---

## 🧠 Insight

> Most systems are read-heavy → optimize reads first

---

# 🧩 2. WRITE-HEAVY SYSTEMS

---

## 💡 Examples
- Logging systems
- Metrics ingestion
- Chat systems

---

## ⚙️ Characteristics
- Writes >> Reads
- High throughput
- Append-heavy

---

## 🏗️ Architecture

User →
- Write buffer / queue
- LSM DB (Cassandra)
- Batch processing

---

## 🚀 Techniques

### 1. LSM Trees
- Sequential writes

---

### 2. Batching
- Combine writes

---

### 3. Partitioning
- Spread writes across shards

---

## ⚠️ Problems

- Read complexity
- Compaction overhead

---

## 🧠 Insight

> Optimize writes using sequential I/O

---

# 🧩 3. FEED / TIMELINE SYSTEMS

---

## 💡 Examples
- Twitter feed
- Instagram feed

---

## ⚙️ Core Problem

> “How to show posts from followed users efficiently?”

---

## 🧩 Two Approaches

---

### 1. Fan-out on Write

---

### 💡 Flow
- User posts → push to followers’ feeds

---

### ✅ Pros
- Fast reads

---

### ❌ Cons
- Expensive writes

---

---

### 2. Fan-out on Read

---

### 💡 Flow
- Fetch posts at read time

---

### ✅ Pros
- Cheap writes

---

### ❌ Cons
- Slow reads

---

---

## 🧠 Insight

> Hybrid approach used in real systems

---

---

# 🧩 4. TIME-SERIES / METRICS SYSTEMS

---

## 💡 Examples
- Monitoring (CPU, memory)
- IoT data

---

## ⚙️ Characteristics
- Append-only
- Time-based queries

---

## 🏗️ Architecture

- Partition by time
- Use LSM-based DB

---

## 🚀 Techniques

- Time bucketing
- Compression
- Retention policies

---

## 🧠 Insight

> Optimize for sequential writes + time queries

---

---

# 🧩 5. SEARCH SYSTEMS

---

## 💡 Examples
- Product search
- Log search

---

## ⚙️ Architecture

Primary DB →
- Stream data →
- Search index (Elasticsearch)

---

## 🚀 Techniques

- Inverted index
- Async indexing

---

## ⚠️ Problems

- Eventual consistency
- Sync lag

---

## 🧠 Insight

> Search DB is NOT source of truth

---

---

# 🧩 6. MULTI-REGION SYSTEMS

---

## 💡 Examples
- Global apps
- SaaS platforms

---

## ⚙️ Challenges

- Latency
- Consistency
- Replication

---

## 🏗️ Architecture

- Geo-replication
- Regional shards

---

## 🚀 Techniques

- Leader per region
- Conflict resolution

---

## 🧠 Insight

> Trade latency vs consistency

---

---

# 🧩 7. CACHE + DB PATTERN (VERY COMMON)

---

## 💡 Flow

Read:
- Check cache
- If miss → DB → update cache

---

## Write:
- Update DB
- Invalidate cache

---

## ⚠️ Hard Problem

> Cache invalidation

---

## 🧠 Insight

> Cache improves performance but adds complexity

---

---

# 🧠 KEY PATTERN SUMMARY

---

| Pattern        | Optimization Focus |
|----------------|------------------|
| Read-heavy     | Cache + replicas |
| Write-heavy    | LSM + batching   |
| Feed           | Fanout strategy  |
| Time-series    | Time partition   |
| Search         | Separate index   |
| Multi-region   | Replication      |

---

---

# 🗣️ INTERVIEW USAGE

---

If asked:

“How would you design X?”

👉 Identify pattern:

- Feed → fanout
- Logs → write-heavy
- Analytics → OLAP

---

---

# 🚨 COMMON MISTAKES

---

- Ignoring workload type
- No caching in read-heavy systems
- Using joins in feed systems
- Not separating search DB
- Mixing OLTP and OLAP

---

---

# 🧠 FINAL INSIGHT

---

> System design = choosing the right pattern, not inventing new ones
