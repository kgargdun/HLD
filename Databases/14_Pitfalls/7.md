# PITFALLS & RED FLAGS (LEVEL 7)

---

## 🎯 GOAL

This section answers:

> “What mistakes should I avoid in interviews and real systems?”

👉 This is what differentiates:
- Average candidates ❌
- Strong candidates ✅

---

## 🧠 CORE IDEA

> Most failures are not due to lack of knowledge  
> They are due to WRONG assumptions

---

# 🚨 CATEGORY 1: DATABASE SELECTION MISTAKES

---

## ❌ 1. Using One Database for Everything

---

### Problem
- Trying to solve all use cases with one DB

---

### Example
- Using only PostgreSQL for:
  - search
  - caching
  - analytics

---

### ✅ Fix
Use:
- SQL → core data
- Redis → cache
- Elasticsearch → search
- OLAP → analytics

---

---

## ❌ 2. Choosing DB Before Access Pattern

---

### Problem
- “Let’s use MongoDB” without justification

---

### ✅ Fix

> Always start with access pattern

---

---

## ❌ 3. Blindly Choosing NoSQL for Scale

---

### Problem
- Thinking NoSQL = scalable by default

---

### Reality
- Trade-offs:
  - weaker consistency
  - complex queries

---

---

## ❌ 4. Ignoring Consistency Requirements

---

### Problem
- Using eventual consistency for critical data

---

### Example
- Payments system → Cassandra ❌

---

### ✅ Fix
- Use strong consistency where needed

---

---

# 🚨 CATEGORY 2: SCALING MISTAKES

---

## ❌ 5. Sharding Too Early

---

### Problem
- Adding sharding without need

---

### ✅ Fix
Scale in order:
1. Vertical scaling  
2. Replication  
3. Sharding  

---

---

## ❌ 6. Bad Shard Key

---

### Problem
- Uneven distribution → hotspots

---

### Example
- Using timestamp → all writes to one shard

---

### ✅ Fix
- Use high-cardinality, evenly distributed keys

---

---

## ❌ 7. Ignoring Hot Partitions

---

### Problem
- One shard overloaded

---

### ✅ Fix
- Monitor + rebalance  
- Use hashing

---

---

# 🚨 CATEGORY 3: QUERY & MODELING MISTAKES

---

## ❌ 8. Overusing Joins at Scale

---

### Problem
- Expensive distributed joins

---

### ✅ Fix
- Denormalize data  

---

---

## ❌ 9. Not Designing for Access Patterns

---

### Problem
- Schema-first thinking

---

### ✅ Fix

> Query-first design

---

---

## ❌ 10. Over-indexing

---

### Problem
- Too many indexes → slow writes

---

### ✅ Fix
- Index only critical queries  

---

---

# 🚨 CATEGORY 4: CACHING MISTAKES

---

## ❌ 11. Cache as Source of Truth

---

### Problem
- Losing data on cache failure

---

### ✅ Fix
- DB is source of truth  

---

---

## ❌ 12. Cache Invalidation Ignored

---

### Problem
- Stale data bugs

---

### ✅ Fix
- Proper invalidation strategy  

---

---

## ❌ 13. Caching Everything

---

### Problem
- Memory waste + complexity

---

### ✅ Fix
- Cache hot data only  

---

---

# 🚨 CATEGORY 5: DISTRIBUTED SYSTEM MISTAKES

---

## ❌ 14. Ignoring Replication Lag

---

### Problem
- Stale reads from replicas

---

### ✅ Fix
- Use leader for critical reads  

---

---

## ❌ 15. Assuming Strong Consistency Everywhere

---

### Problem
- System becomes slow/unavailable

---

### ✅ Fix
- Use eventual consistency where possible  

---

---

## ❌ 16. Using 2PC at Scale

---

### Problem
- Blocking, latency, poor scalability

---

### ✅ Fix
- Use sagas  

---

---

## ❌ 17. Ignoring Failure Scenarios

---

### Problem
- Happy-path design only

---

### ✅ Fix
- Design for:
  - retries
  - idempotency
  - partial failures  

---

---

# 🚨 CATEGORY 6: PERFORMANCE MISTAKES

---

## ❌ 18. Ignoring Disk I/O

---

### Problem
- Slow queries due to I/O

---

### ✅ Fix
- Use indexes + caching  

---

---

## ❌ 19. Running Heavy Queries on OLTP

---

### Problem
- System slowdown

---

### ✅ Fix
- Use OLAP systems  

---

---

## ❌ 20. No Backpressure / Rate Limiting

---

### Problem
- System overload

---

### ✅ Fix
- Add throttling  

---

---

# 🧠 INTERVIEW SUPER-POWER (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

## If you say this → HUGE SIGNAL

---

“I would avoid distributed transactions and instead design the system using eventual consistency with idempotent operations.”

---

---

## Or:

---

“I would design schema based on access patterns and avoid joins in high-scale paths.”

---

---

## Or:

---

“I would start simple and only shard when needed.”

---

---

# 🧠 FINAL MENTAL MODEL

---

## Good design:

- Based on constraints  
- Evolves with scale  
- Uses multiple systems  

---

## Bad design:

- Overengineered  
- No trade-off discussion  
- One-size-fits-all  

---

---

# 🔥 FINAL INSIGHT (MOST IMPORTANT)

---

> System design is not about building perfect systems  
> It is about avoiding catastrophic mistakes
