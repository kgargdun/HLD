# 🌐 2.1 REPLICATION (LEADER–FOLLOWER, SYNC vs ASYNC)

---

## 📘 Definition (textbook-style)

**Replication** is the process of copying and maintaining the same data across multiple database nodes to improve availability, fault tolerance, and read scalability.

---

## 💡 Intuition (Why it exists)

Single database node:

* ❌ Can fail → system down
* ❌ Limited read capacity
* ❌ Geographic latency

👉 Solution:

> “Keep multiple copies of data across nodes”

---

## 🧩 Why Replication is Needed

---

### 1. High Availability

* Node failure → system still works

---

### 2. Read Scalability

* Distribute read traffic

---

### 3. Fault Tolerance

* Data not lost if one node crashes

---

### 4. Geo-distribution

* Serve users from nearest region

---

## 🧩 Replication Models

---

# 1. 🧭 LEADER–FOLLOWER (Primary–Replica)

---

## 📘 Definition

One node (**leader**) handles writes, others (**followers**) replicate data.

---

## 🔄 Flow

1. Client writes → leader
2. Leader updates data
3. Leader sends changes to followers
4. Followers apply updates

---

### 🧠 Key Insight

> Single source of truth → simpler consistency

---

## 🌍 Real-world Mapping

* PostgreSQL
* MySQL

---

## ✅ Pros

* Simple design
* Strong consistency (if configured)
* Easy to reason

---

## ❌ Cons

* Leader is bottleneck
* Failover complexity
* Replication lag possible

---

# 2. 🔀 MULTI-LEADER (Multi-Master)

---

## 📘 Definition

Multiple nodes accept writes and replicate to each other.

---

## 💡 Example

* Multi-region apps
* Each region has its own leader

---

## ⚠️ Problem

👉 Write conflicts

* Same data updated in different nodes

---

## 🌍 Real-world Mapping

* Cassandra (conceptually similar patterns)

---

## ✅ Pros

* High availability
* Geo-distributed writes

---

## ❌ Cons

* Conflict resolution required
* Complex system

---

# 3. 🚫 LEADERLESS (QUORUM-BASED)

---

## 📘 Definition

No single leader — any node can accept reads/writes.

---

## 💡 How it works

* Writes sent to multiple nodes
* Reads fetch from multiple nodes

---

### 🧠 Quorum Concept

* Write to N nodes
* Read from M nodes

Ensure:

> R + W > N → consistency

---

## 🌍 Real-world Mapping

* DynamoDB
* Cassandra

---

## ✅ Pros

* No single point of failure
* High availability

---

## ❌ Cons

* Eventual consistency
* Complex conflict resolution

---

# ⚡ Sync vs Async Replication (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

## 🔹 Synchronous Replication

---

### 📘 Definition

Write is successful only after replicas confirm

---

### ✅ Pros

* Strong consistency
* No data loss

---

### ❌ Cons

* High latency
* Slower writes

---

---

## 🔹 Asynchronous Replication

---

### 📘 Definition

Leader responds before replicas are updated

---

### ✅ Pros

* Low latency
* High throughput

---

### ❌ Cons

* Replication lag
* Possible data loss on crash

---

## ⚖️ Sync vs Async (IMPORTANT)

| Feature        | Sync Replication | Async Replication |
| -------------- | ---------------- | ----------------- |
| Latency        | High             | Low               |
| Consistency    | Strong           | Eventual          |
| Data loss risk | None             | Possible          |

---

## 🎯 When to use what (Decision Thinking)

---

### Use Leader–Follower when:

* Strong consistency needed
* Moderate scale

---

### Use Multi-leader when:

* Multi-region writes
* Need high availability

---

### Use Leaderless when:

* Massive scale
* Eventual consistency acceptable

---

### Use Sync replication when:

* Financial systems
* Critical data

---

### Use Async replication when:

* Performance critical
* Some inconsistency acceptable

---

## 🧠 Key Insight (INTERVIEW GOLD)

> “Replication improves availability and scalability but introduces consistency challenges, especially with asynchronous or multi-leader setups.”

---

## 🗣️ Interview One-liner

> “Replication copies data across nodes for availability and scalability, with trade-offs between consistency and latency depending on the replication model and sync strategy.”

---

## 🚨 Red Flags / Mistakes

* ❌ Assuming replicas are always up-to-date
* ❌ Ignoring replication lag
* ❌ Using async replication for critical data blindly
* ❌ Not planning failover
* ❌ Ignoring conflict resolution in multi-leader

---

## 🔥 Interview Insight (VERY IMPORTANT)

👉 Most production systems:

* Leader–Follower
* Async replication
* Read replicas

👉 Because:

> Balance between performance and consistency

---

## ✍️ Quick Mental Model

> Replication = copies of data
>
> Sync → safe but slow
> Async → fast but risky
