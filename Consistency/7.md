# 📄 **File 7 — Practical System Design Patterns (Consistency in Interviews)**

## 🧠 1. The Golden Rule (MOST IMPORTANT)

> “Consistency is not chosen globally — it is chosen per feature”

---

### ❌ Wrong thinking:

> “System is strongly consistent”

### ✅ Correct thinking:

* Payments → strong consistency
* Feed → eventual consistency
* Chat → causal consistency

---

👉 This alone differentiates strong candidates.

# ⚙️ 3. Choosing Consistency (Framework)

---

## Step 1: Ask

> “What happens if data is wrong or stale?”

---

## Step 2: Classify

| Impact                      | Consistency |
| --------------------------- | ----------- |
| Critical (money, inventory) | Strong      |
| Order matters               | Causal      |
| Slight delay OK             | Eventual    |

---

## Step 3: Apply

* Strong → leader + consensus
* Eventual → replication + quorum
* Causal → vector clocks

---

---

# 🧠 5. Caching + Consistency (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

## Problem

Cache introduces:

* stale data

---

## Patterns

---

### 5.1 Write-Through Cache

* Write DB + cache together

✔ Consistent
❌ Slower

---

### 5.2 Write-Back Cache

* Write cache first

✔ Fast
❌ Risk of data loss

---

### 5.3 Cache Invalidation

> “Hardest problem in distributed systems”

---

## Interview Line

> “We use cache invalidation or TTL to balance freshness and performance.”

---

---

# ⚠️ 8. Pitfalls & Red Flags (HIGH SIGNAL)

---

## 🚫 Red Flag 1:

> “We’ll use strong consistency everywhere”

👉 Bad:

* Not scalable
* High latency

---

## 🚫 Red Flag 2:

> “Eventual consistency is fine for payments”

👉 Wrong:

* Leads to money issues

---

## 🚫 Red Flag 3:

> Ignoring replica lag

👉 Always mention:

* “Reads from replicas may be stale”

---

## 🚫 Red Flag 4:

> No conflict resolution strategy

👉 Must mention:

* LWW / vector clocks / merge

---

# 🎯 9. Ready-Made Answer Templates

---

## Template 1 — General

> “We choose consistency based on business requirements. Critical operations like payments use strong consistency via leader-based replication, while high-scale features like feeds use eventual consistency with quorum and conflict resolution.”

---

---

## Template 2 — Dynamo Style

> “We use leaderless replication with N replicas and quorum (R + W > N). Conflicts are resolved using vector clocks and application-level merging.”

---

---

## Template 3 — Strong System

> “We use a leader-based system with Raft consensus to ensure linearizability. Writes are committed after majority acknowledgment.”

---

---

## Template 4 — Hybrid

> “We use strong consistency for writes, eventual consistency for reads via replicas, and caching for performance.”

# 🧠 10. Final Mental Model (ULTIMATE)

---

👉 Every system decision = answer 3 questions:

---

### 1. Can data be stale?

* Yes → eventual
* No → strong

---

### 2. Does order matter?

* Yes → causal
* No → eventual

---

### 3. Can conflicts happen?

* Yes → need resolution
* No → simpler system

---

---

# 🔚 Final Summary

---

* Consistency is **feature-specific**
* Use:

  * Strong → critical data
  * Causal → ordered data
  * Eventual → scalable systems
* Real systems = hybrid
* Always mention:

  * tradeoffs
  * failure scenarios
  * conflict handling
