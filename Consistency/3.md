# 📄 **File 3 — Replication & How Consistency is Achieved**

---

## 🧠 1. Why Replication Exists

Replication = storing **multiple copies of data across machines**

---

### Why do we need it?

1. **Availability**

   * If one node dies → system still works

2. **Scalability**

   * Reads can be served from multiple nodes

3. **Latency**

   * Serve data from nearest region

---

### Real systems:

* Amazon → multi-region replication
* Netflix → global data replication
* Google Spanner → strongly consistent global replication

---

## ⚠️ Core Problem

> Once you have multiple copies → how do you keep them consistent?

This is the **heart of distributed systems**

---

# 🔁 2. Replication Models (3 Types)

---

## 🥇 2.1 Leader–Follower (Primary–Replica)

---

### Idea

* One node = **Leader** (writes)
* Others = **Followers** (replicate)

---

### Flow

1. Client → Leader (write)
2. Leader updates data
3. Leader replicates to followers
4. Followers apply update

---

### Read Options

* From leader → **strong consistency**
* From follower → **possible stale data**

---

### Real Systems

* PostgreSQL
* MySQL
* Amazon RDS

---

## Tradeoffs

* ✔ Simple
* ✔ Strong consistency possible
* ❌ Leader bottleneck
* ❌ Single point of failure (if no failover)

---

## Interview Line

> “We use leader-based replication for strong consistency by routing all writes through a primary node.”

---

# 🥈 2.2 Multi-Leader Replication

---

### Idea

* Multiple leaders accept writes
* Leaders sync with each other

---

### Flow

* Region A leader ←→ Region B leader

---

### Why use?

* Geo-distributed systems
* Reduce latency for writes

---

### Problem

👉 Conflicts!

Example:

* User updates profile in US
* Same user updates in India

---

### Real Systems

* CouchDB
* MongoDB (multi-region setups)

---

## Tradeoffs

* ✔ Low latency writes
* ✔ High availability
* ❌ Conflict resolution needed
* ❌ Complex

---

## Interview Line

> “Multi-leader improves write latency but introduces conflict resolution complexity.”

---

# 🥉 2.3 Leaderless Replication (Dynamo Style)

---

### Idea

* No leader
* Any node can accept writes

---

### Flow

1. Client sends write to multiple nodes
2. Nodes independently store data
3. System reconciles later

---

### Real Systems

* Apache Cassandra
* Amazon DynamoDB

---

## Key Concept

👉 Uses:

* Quorum (we’ll cover next file)
* Eventual consistency

---

## Tradeoffs

* ✔ Highly available
* ✔ No single bottleneck
* ❌ Conflicts common
* ❌ Complex reads

---

## Interview Line

> “Leaderless systems rely on quorum and eventual consistency for high availability.”

---

# ⚡ 3. Sync vs Async Replication (CRITICAL)

---

## 🟢 Synchronous Replication

---

### Flow

* Leader waits for followers to confirm before responding

---

### Result

✔ Strong consistency
❌ Higher latency

---

### Example

* Google Spanner

---

## 🔵 Asynchronous Replication

---

### Flow

* Leader responds immediately
* Followers updated later

---

### Result

✔ Fast
✔ Available
❌ Replica lag

---

### Example

* MySQL
* PostgreSQL

---

## Interview Insight

> “Async replication introduces replication lag, which leads to eventual consistency.”

---

# ⏳ 4. Replica Lag (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

## What is it?

Delay between:

* write on leader
* update on follower

---

## Why it matters?

👉 Causes:

* stale reads
* inconsistent user experience

---

### Example

* User updates profile
* Reads from follower → sees old data

---

## Real-world impact

* Instagram → stale likes
* Amazon → cart inconsistency (must avoid)

---

## Mitigation

* Read from leader
* Session consistency
* Read-after-write guarantees

---

# 🔧 5. Failure Scenarios (INTERVIEW FAVORITE)

---

## Case 1: Leader crashes

👉 What happens?

* System must elect new leader

---

## Real Systems

* Raft Consensus Algorithm
* Paxos Algorithm

---

## Case 2: Network partition

* Some nodes isolated

👉 Leads to:

* split brain
* inconsistent writes

---

## Case 3: Replica falls behind

👉 Causes:

* stale reads
* inconsistent responses

# 🧠 7. Mental Model (IMPORTANT)

---

### Think like this:

👉 Replication creates:

* Conflicts

👉 Consistency defines:

# 🎯 8. How to Use in Interviews

---

## Example Question:

“How do you ensure consistency in your system?”

---

### Answer structure:

1. Replication strategy

   > “We use leader-follower replication”

2. Consistency choice

   > “Writes go to leader for strong consistency”

3. Optimization

   > “Reads can go to followers for scalability”

4. Tradeoff

   > “This may cause stale reads due to replica lag”

---

# 🔥 9. Common Mistakes

---

### ❌ “Replication = consistency”

✔ Truth:

> Replication creates inconsistency problems

---

### ❌ “More replicas = better consistency”

✔ Truth:

> More replicas = more coordination needed

---

### ❌ “Async replication is fine everywhere”

✔ Truth:

> Not for critical data (payments, inventory)

---

# 🔚 Final Summary

* Replication = multiple copies of data
* 3 types:

  * leader-follower
  * multi-leader
  * leaderless
* Sync vs async = key tradeoff
* Replica lag = main problem
* Systems use:

  * read repair
  * anti-entropy
* Failures define real behavior
