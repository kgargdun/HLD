# 📄 **File 6 — Consensus & Strong Guarantees**

---

## 🧠 1. What is Consensus?

> Consensus = all nodes agree on the **same value in the same order**

---

### Why do we need it?

Because in distributed systems:

* Multiple nodes exist
* Failures happen
* Messages are delayed

👉 Without consensus:

* Different nodes may accept different writes
* System diverges

---

### Real-world need:

* Leader election
* Log replication
* Metadata storage

---

### Real Systems

* ZooKeeper
* etcd
* Google Spanner

---

## ⚠️ 2. Why Quorum Alone is NOT Enough

From File 4:

👉 Quorum ensures overlap
BUT:

* Does not prevent **concurrent writes**
* Does not ensure **single leader**

---

### Problem

Two nodes think they are leaders:

👉 Both accept writes
👉 Conflict

---

👉 This is where **consensus** comes in

---

# 🏛️ 3. Core Idea of Consensus

---

System guarantees:

1. Only **one leader**
2. All writes go through leader
3. All nodes apply writes in **same order**

---

👉 This gives:

✔ Strong consistency
✔ Linearizability

---

# ⚙️ 5. Raft Consensus Algorithm (MUST KNOW)

---

## 🧩 Intuition

Raft = **easy-to-understand consensus**

Breaks into:

1. Leader election
2. Log replication
3. Safety guarantees

---

---

## 🥇 5.1 Leader Election

---

### Flow

1. All nodes start as **followers**
2. Timeout → become **candidate**
3. Request votes
4. Majority wins → becomes leader

---

👉 Uses quorum (majority)

---

## Key Insight

> Only majority can elect leader → prevents split brain

---

---

## 🥈 5.2 Log Replication

---

### Idea

Leader maintains a **log of operations**

---

### Flow

1. Client → leader (write)
2. Leader appends to log
3. Sends to followers
4. Followers acknowledge
5. Leader commits (after majority)

---

👉 Then applied to DB

---

## Example

```id="z2qz7j"
SET x = 10
SET x = 20
```

All nodes apply in **same order**

---

---

## 🥉 5.3 Safety Guarantees

---

### Guarantees

✔ No two leaders at same time
✔ Logs are consistent
✔ No committed data is lost

---

---

## 🔥 Interview Line

> “Raft ensures strong consistency by enforcing a single leader and replicating a log with majority acknowledgment.”

# 💥 7. Failure Handling (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

## Case 1: Leader crashes

---

### What happens?

* Followers detect timeout
* New election starts
* New leader chosen

---

## Case 2: Network partition

---

Split into:

* Majority group
* Minority group

---

### Behavior

* Majority → continues
* Minority → cannot elect leader ❌

---

👉 Prevents split brain

---

## Key Insight

> Only majority group is active

---

---

## Case 3: Uncommitted logs

---

Leader crashes before commit

👉 New leader:

* Discards uncommitted entries
* Ensures consistency

---

---

# 🔄 8. Exactly-Once vs At-Least-Once

---

## At-Least-Once

* Retries possible
* Duplicate writes possible

---

## Exactly-Once

* Hard in distributed systems
* Needs:

  * idempotency
  * deduplication

---

---

## Real Systems

* Kafka
* Stripe (idempotency keys)
